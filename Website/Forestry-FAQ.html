FAQ


1. What is CBD?

CBD stands for cannabidiol, which is a naturally occurring compound found in the cannabis plant. Unlike THC, another well-known compound found in cannabis, CBD is not psychoactive, which means it does not produce the "high" typically associated with marijuana use.

2. Is CBD legal in the US?

CBD derived from hemp, a strain of the cannabis plant that contains less than 0.3% THC, was federally legalized in 2018 under the Agricultural Improvement Act, also known as the Farm Bill. This means that CBD products derived from hemp are legal at the federal level.

3. What are the benefits of CBD?

CBD has been studied for its potential therapeutic benefits including reducing anxiety, pain relief, improving sleep, neuroprotective effects, and acne fighting properties.

4. Does CBD get you high?

No, CBD does not get you high. Unlike THC, which is the psychoactive compound found in cannabis that produces the "high" sensation, CBD is not psychoactive and does not produce an intoxicating effect.

5. How is CBD different from hemp oil?

CBD is a compound found in the cannabis plant, and it is one of many cannabinoids. Hemp oil, on the other hand, is a product made from the seeds of the hemp plant. CBD is extracted from the flowers, leaves, and stalks of the hemp plant, while hemp oil is made by cold- pressing the seeds of the hemp plant. CBD is often used for its potential health benefits, such as reducing anxiety and inflammation, while hemp oil is often used as a dietary supplement or in cosmetic products. While both CBD and hemp oil come from the same plant, they are different products with different uses and properties.

6. What are the different types of CBD products?

There are many different types of CBD products available on the market today. Some of the most common types are: tinctures, topicals, capsules, edibles, vape cartridges, and isolate powder.

7. Are there any side effects of using CBD?

While CBD is considered safe, It's important to note that CBD can interact with certain medications, including blood thinners, antidepressants, and antipsychotics. If you are taking any medication, it's important to talk to your healthcare provider before using CBD to avoid potential interactions.

8. How long does it take for CBD to take effect?

The time it takes for CBD to take effect can vary depending on a number of factors, including the individual's body weight, metabolism, and the method of consumption. On average, it can take anywhere from 30 minutes to 1 hour to feel the effects of CBD when taken orally. The effects of inhaled or topical CBD are typically felt within minutes of use.

9. How long does the effect of CBD last?

The duration of the effects of CBD can vary depending on a number of factors, including the individual's body weight, metabolism, and dosage. However, the effects can typically last anywhere from 2 to 4 hours on average.

10. Can CBD interact with other medications?

Yes, CBD can interact with other medications in some cases. CBD is metabolized by the liver using the same enzymes as many prescription and over-the-counter medications, which can lead to interactions. CBD can increase or decrease the effectiveness of other medications by affecting how they are metabolized in the body.

11. What is the difference between full-spectrum and isolate CBD?

The main difference between the two is the presence of other cannabinoids and plant compounds in full spectrum CBD products. Full spectrum CBD contains a range of other cannabinoids, terpenes, and flavonoids that are naturally present in the cannabis plant. CBD isolate, on the other hand, is a pure form of cannabidiol that has been isolated from all other plant compounds. This means that it contains only CBD and none of the other cannabinoids or terpenes found in the cannabis plant.

12. What is THC?

THC (tetrahydrocannabinol) is the main psychoactive compound found in the cannabis plant. It is the compound responsible for producing the "high" or euphoric effect associated with marijuana use. THC works by binding to cannabinoid receptors in the brain and central nervous system, causing changes in mood, perception, and cognitive function.

13. What are the health benefits of THC?

THC has been studied for its potential health benefits including: pain relief, reduced vomiting, appetite stimulation, improved sleep, and treating anxiety and depression.

14. Is THC legal in the US?

The legal status of THC in the US depends on several factors, including the state in which it is being used and the purpose for which it is being used. In some states, THC is legal for medicinal purposes with a valid prescription or recommendation from a healthcare provider. In a smaller number of states, THC is also legal for recreational use by adults aged 21 or older. However, the specific regulations and requirements vary by state.

15. What types of THC products are available?

There are many different types of THC products, including dried flower, edibles, tinctures, concentrates, and topicals. Each has its own unique method of consumption and effect.

16. How is THC different from CBD?

THC and CBD are two of the most well-known compounds found in the cannabis plant, but they have different effects on the body and mind. Mainly, THC is psychoactive, meaning it can produce a "high" while CBD, on the other hand, is not.

17. Are there any medical uses for THC?

Yes, THC has been used to treat a variety of medical conditions, such as chronic pain, nausea, and muscle spasms. It's important to consult with a healthcare professional before using THC for medical purposes.

18. Does THC have any side effects?

THC can have side effects at higher doses or when consumed frequently. Some common side effects of THC include: dry mouth, reed eyes, increased heart rate, or anxiety.

19. How long does the effect of THC last?

The duration of the effects of THC can vary depending on several factors, including the dose, method of consumption, and individual factors such as tolerance and metabolism. In general, the effects of THC can last from 2 to 6 hours.

20. How should cannabis products be stored?

Cannabis products, should be stored properly to help preserve their potency and freshness. Here are some general tips for storing your products: keep away from heat and light, store in airtight containers, and avoid moisture.
